# SEO Strategy

## 1. SEO philosophy

Nexus should not be a thin AI content site. Programmatic SEO should be powered by real structured data, useful templates, and human-reviewed quality.

The aim is to capture long-tail, high-intent search demand while giving users a faster answer than traditional articles.

## 5. Page quality rules

A page should not be published/indexable unless it has:

- Useful unique data
- Clear verification status
- Search intent match
- Internal links
- Last updated date
- No fake filler
- Good mobile layout
- Related action: map/search/AI/guide

## 6. Internal linking

Every entity page should link to:

- Related map markers
- Related guides
- Category page
- Related entities
- AI ask action

Every guide should link to:

- Entities mentioned
- Map markers
- Related guides
- Progress tracker if relevant

## 7. Structured data

Potential schema types:

- WebPage
- FAQPage
- BreadcrumbList
- ItemList
- VideoObject where embedded video exists
- HowTo where appropriate and safe

## 8. Freshness strategy

- Last updated date visible.
- Last verified date visible for factual pages.
- Patch-affected pages flagged.
- High-traffic pages reviewed first after updates.

## 9. Search Console feedback loop

Use GSC to find:

- Pages with impressions but low CTR.
- Queries with no matching page.
- Ranking pages needing better data.
- Long-tail opportunity clusters.

## 10. SEO risks

- Thin pages.
- Duplicate pages.
- Overreliance on AI text.
- Publishing speculation as fact.
- Copying competitor data.
- Indexing low-quality user submissions.

## 11. SEO acceptance criteria

- Sitemap auto-generates from published pages.
- Canonicals correct.
- Noindex applied to weak/speculative pages where appropriate.
- Metadata generated but human-reviewable.
- Internal links generated from graph relationships.
- Analytics tracks organic landing page behaviour.

# Content Engine

## 1. Content philosophy

Content should be a view over verified data.

Instead of manually writing every article, Nexus should create structured templates that combine:

- Verified entity data
- Relationships
- Map markers
- Guide notes
- FAQs
- Source status
- Last verified date

## 2. Content types

### Direct answer pages

Short pages that answer one intent clearly.

Example:

- Where to find [vehicle]
- How to unlock [weapon]
- Best business for beginners

### Database pages

Entity pages with structured data.

### Guide pages

Human-edited walkthroughs or explainers.

### Ranking pages

Automatically updated from structured stats plus editorial rules.

### Patch/update explainers

Summaries of what changed and which pages/records are affected.

### Community discovery pages

Approved discoveries with contributor credit.

## 3. Template anatomy

Every content page should include:

- Direct answer near top
- Status/verification label
- Data cards
- Map action if relevant
- Steps or comparison
- Related links
- FAQ
- Sources/last verified

## 4. Human review model

AI drafts:

- Titles
- Metadata
- FAQs
- First-pass copy
- Internal links

Humans approve:

- Material facts
- Rankings
- Recommendations
- Public pages
- Sensitive/legal content

## 5. Voice and tone

Tone should be:

- Clear
- Useful
- Confident when verified
- Honest when uncertain
- Friendly but not cringe
- Short enough for mobile

Avoid:

- Clickbait
- Overhype
- Fake certainty
- Excessive slang
- Long intros

## 6. Update workflow

1. Data record changes.
2. Related pages flagged.
3. AI drafts changes.
4. Admin reviews.
5. Page publishes.
6. Search/embeddings/sitemap update.
7. Analytics monitors.

## 7. Editorial standards

- First paragraph answers the main question.
- No filler history unless it helps.
- Spoilers controlled.
- Verification state visible.
- Conflicting info acknowledged.
- Links should be useful, not just SEO-driven.

## 8. Content backlog inputs

- Failed searches
- AI unknown answers
- Search Console queries
- Community submissions
- Patch notes
- Social trends
- Competitor gaps
- High-traffic page improvements

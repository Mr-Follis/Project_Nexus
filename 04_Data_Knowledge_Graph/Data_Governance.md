# Data Governance

## 1. Purpose

Nexus will only work if users trust the data. Data governance defines how information enters, changes, gets verified, and is removed or marked outdated.

## 2. Data source policy

Allowed:

- Official game publisher/developer pages.
- Official trailers/screenshots/media.
- Direct gameplay verification after release.
- Community submissions with evidence.
- Public creator content used as a research lead, with original verification or attribution where appropriate.
- Editorial research.

Avoid:

- Publishing confidential leaks.
- Copying competitor databases.
- Reproducing full copyrighted guides.
- Using AI-generated facts without verification.
- Presenting speculation as confirmed.

## 3. Verification lifecycle

1. Draft/speculative.
2. Evidence added.
3. Admin/editor reviews.
4. Published with status.
5. Community can report corrections.
6. Patch/update can mark stale.
7. Record is reverified or marked outdated.

## 4. Verification labels

### Confirmed official

From official publisher/developer source.

### Confirmed gameplay

Verified directly in gameplay by Nexus/editorial team.

### Community verified

Multiple credible submissions or trusted contributors confirm it.

### Likely

Strong evidence but not fully confirmed.

### Speculative

Unverified. Must be visually labelled and excluded from AI factual answers unless user asks for speculation.

### Outdated

Known or suspected to have changed.

### Rejected

False, duplicate, spam, or invalid.

## 5. AI data rules

AI may:

- Extract possible facts.
- Suggest fields.
- Summarise sources.
- Group duplicates.
- Draft guide copy.
- Generate SEO metadata.

AI may not:

- Publish material facts directly.
- Upgrade verification status to confirmed without approval.
- Invent missing stats.
- Remove source uncertainty.

## 6. Staleness rules

Records should be reviewed when:

- Patch notes mention related entities.
- Users report outdated information.
- AI detects conflicting submissions.
- A high-traffic page has not been verified recently.
- A major update/DLC launches.

## 7. Legal/copyright notes

- Always use an unofficial fan-site disclaimer.
- Avoid implying affiliation with Rockstar or other publishers.
- Do not use logos, screenshots, or artwork in ways that violate rights.
- Prefer original artwork, licensed assets, embeds, and user-submitted assets with permission.
- Avoid copying page structures/data sets directly from competitors.

## 8. Moderation rules

Reject submissions that:

- Include hate/abuse.
- Include illegal or unsafe real-world instructions.
- Are spam or self-promotion.
- Claim false official access.
- Include copyrighted uploads without rights.
- Are duplicates without added value.

## 9. Source reliability score

Suggested score:

- 95–100 official source
- 90–100 direct Nexus gameplay verification
- 75–90 trusted contributor with evidence
- 50–75 normal user submission with proof
- 25–50 unsupported third-party claim
- 0–25 anonymous rumour/no evidence

## 10. Data governance acceptance criteria

- Every published fact has a source or clear status.
- Every major data change has audit history.
- AI-generated content is labelled in admin before approval.
- Users can report outdated/wrong data.
- Admin can see stale/high-risk records.
